Evidence That Nighttime Baths Can Be Good:
1. Improved Sleep Quality and Faster Sleep Onset:
Biological cooling effect: Warm baths or showers taken 1-2 hours before bedtime help trigger the body's natural cooling—key for falling asleep faster and sleeping better.
A systematic review found that even 10-minute warm baths (40-42 °C) before bed improve sleep efficiency and reduce time to fall asleep.
Popular Mechanics explains that the warming effect promotes heat loss when you exit the bath, aligning with the circadian rhythm and aiding sleep.
Verywell Health confirms that warming the skin before bed accelerates the core-temperature drop and improves sleep quality.
Time.com notes that evening showers support sleep, reduce sleep onset, and enhance rest when timed and heated properly.

2. Relaxation and Better Sleep Hygiene:
 A warm bath can help relax muscles, ease stress, and act as a calming bedtime routine or signal to start winding down.
A structured nighttime ritual that includes bathing may support your body's sleep cues and improve overall nightly rest.
